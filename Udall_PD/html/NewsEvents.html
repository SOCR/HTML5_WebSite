June 30, 2016 Dr. Kelvin Chou, MD interviewed by Neurology Today in Fall Episodes and Frequency in Early Parkinson’s Disease. The report discussed a longitudinal study of falls in 1,741 patients with early Parkinson's disease. At baseline, 132 Subjects treated with dopaminergic agents for at least three months and no more than two years reported a fall. Three to six years later, 691 people (40 percent) said they had had a fall since their baseline assessment. 95 of the 132 people (72 percent) who reported a fall at the start of the study subsequently had another fall.
